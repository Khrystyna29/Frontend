Macarons

One of the most famous desserts in the country. These delicious cakes are made with almond flour, egg whites, powdered sugar, granulated sugar and food coloring. The shell of a successful pasta should be both crispy and easy to melt in the mouth. The filling, be it jam, butter cream or ganache, should always be in sufficient quantity! Traditionally popular macaron flavors were chocolate, vanilla and caramel. Today, pastry chefs are very creative and created from selling products from all over the world to offer original macarons in a variety of colors and flavors.

Opéra

Opéra is an exquisite pastry that combines two famous flavors - chocolate and coffee - into one luxurious cake, saturated with richness and aroma. The cake consists of layers of almond sponge cake (known as Joconde sponge cake in French), soaked in coffee syrup, sandwiched between alternating layers of coffee buttercream and chocolate ganache, and covered in chocolate frosting.

Lemon tart with meringue

A true favorite of the French, tarte au citron meringuée, or lemon meringue tart, is undoubtedly one of the most popular and beloved desserts in the world. The lemon meringue tart is essentially a development of the original tarte au citron (lemon tart). Tarte au citron is a shortbread filled with lemon curd made from egg yolks, lemon zest, juice and sugar. Tarte au citron meringueée takes this classic recipe and adds a top layer of short-baked, tender and airy meringue.

Croissant

A croissant is a symbolic pastry that is traditionally served in the morning for breakfast with coffee or hot chocolate. In France, it is customary to eat it plain or, for real gourmets, cut it in half, lightly fry it and spread it with butter and jam or chocolate paste! Croissant can be found in any bakery (French: boulangerie) throughout the country! It is made from puff pastry containing yeast. According to the French confectioner Christophe Felder, to prepare a perfect croissant, you need to master 50 parameters!
